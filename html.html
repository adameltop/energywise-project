EnergyWise: We Should Use Renewable Resources

Energy Sources

Energy can come from both renewable and non-renewable sources. Understanding the differences and impacts is crucial to making informed energy decisions.

Renewable Energy:

  • Solar: Harnessing the power of the sun to generate electricity.
  • Wind: Wind turbines convert kinetic energy into electrical power.
  • Hydro: Using flowing water to produce energy, often through dams.

Non-Renewable Energy:

  • Coal: A fossil fuel that is burned to produce electricity but has high environmental costs.
  • Natural Gas: A cleaner alternative to coal but still a major source of CO2 emissions.
  • Oil: A widely used energy source that contributes significantly to pollution and climate change.
